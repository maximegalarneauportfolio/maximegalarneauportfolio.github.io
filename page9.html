''Welcome Home''

Created in Unity

 

Constraints

- Created a level following a rational level design document
- Introduced new ingredients and mechanics use for the rest of the game

 

Goals

- Create the third level of the game  in collaboration with a Level Artist
- Adjust the difficulty to ensure a progression in the challenge

 

Walkthrought Duration

approximately 2m30s

 

Production Time

14 weeks

Previous Next

''Welcome Home'' is the third level of the game HIVE developped at the Campus ADN with Unity in only 14 weeks.

The player explores a Hunter's Manor which is more a memorial of his past hunts. While exploring, the player will discover that the owner of this mansion does not only hunt normal creatures but mythical ones as well. 

There's an evolution with the taxidermied beasts in addition to the paintings on display in order to raise the tension until the last revelation.

Design Intentions

Teach & Practice

This level has many introductions of new ingredients used during the rest of the game. Simple but diverse patterns are essential to help the players understand how they work and how they can interact with them. For instance, I used the Lasers in all the way possible in my level: static, intermittent, moving horizontally & vertically. These patterns must be simple but effective. 

Landmark

I didn't want a fully linear path for my level design, the players come back 3 times in the same room. That's why this central room had to have a big noticeable landmark to ensure players recognize where they are each time they come back in it. From all the entrances, it was important to frame the landmark perfectly.

Rewarding Exploration

The level design included a lot of small spaces to create the impression of an inhabited station. This feeling is greatly increased by the Level Artist. I used those spaces to hide collectibles in order to reward the explorer and completionist players.

Rythm

This game has two main gameplay elements which is shooting and platforming. Great care was taken to let some spaces between these sections of combats and jumping to let the players breath. I didn't want to overburden the players with too many challenges for it's only the third level.
