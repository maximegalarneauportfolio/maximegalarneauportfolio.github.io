USED WATERS

TEAM FORTRESS 2
Created in HAMMER 

Level Design & Level Art by Maxime Galarneau
All assets were free and from Team Fortress 2 and Half-Life 2

Used Waters

Created in HAMMER, Valve's Editor

 

Constraints

- Asymmetric map with different advantages and disadvantages for both teams
- Must have outdoor and indoor environments

 

Goals

6v6 CTF multiplayer map

 

Walkthrough Duration

Approximately 12min per match

 

Production Time

50 hours

PreviousNextClose


Used Waters is a multiplayer map in the universe of Team Fortress developped at the Campus ADN with Hammer.

The Blu and Red teams fight each other in an old Water Treatment Plant to get to the Intels first. The Blu Team starts at the exterior. They are close to open areas like the Docking Bay and the Warehouse. The Red Team starts inside the building, which is closer to smaller rooms like the Labs and the Offices.

Both teams will mostly fight in the Basin where they can shoot each other on the narrow footbridges or underwater.

Design Intentions

Landmarks

To ensure players can recognize where they are in the map, great care was taken to have a distinct elements in each room. The shelves in the Warehouse have uniquely colored barrels stored on each of them to facilate their recognition. Numbers are also painted on the floor to identifiy the aisles and increase spacial awareness.

Guidance

It's easy to get lost in a new multiplayer map, that's why all the EXIT signs have a red light in the dimmer environments to make sure players notice where they can escape the rooms. Many Information Pannels with arrows will also guide the players towards their objectives but without holding their hands all the time.  

Diversity

To obtain the Intel, each team has many choices to reach and avoid rooms defended by their opponents. Each section has at least 3 exits, some are short but risky and others are safer but take longer to cross. The Basin which is in the middle of the map, contains the only door connecting both halves of the level creating a much needed chokepoint to ensure confrontation between the 2 teams.

Space to Fight

I quickly realised that my initial design for this multiplayer map was more appropriate for a Call of Duty or Counter Strike game. Team Fortress needs space to allow the players to have fun with the great variety of abilities and weapons available. Cover and optional rooms were cut to ensure a better experience and create more opportunities for the players to see each other.

Strategy

This map has open space to create great combat between teams. There's also other ways to get around, all characters can navigate and climb on the shelves with discretion in order to ambush their opponents. In the Basin, players can jump into the water to become invisible from the surface. It's a good way to escape a tough fight or go around their opponents to suprise them from behind. 

Initial Design in SketchUp

My initial design had more exterior space and an underground tunnel where you could reach the Basin from a small pool outside. The Basin was also much larger. Once in Hammer, I realised that the players could spend too much time looking for each other, instead of shooting.

Final Design in Hammer

To ensure more confrontations between the players, the Basin's size was reduced and a chokepoint  became the only way to join the 2 halves of the map. The idea of an underground tunnel stayed but only to get to the 2 outside pools to escape a fight. There's not much cover out there, there is still a great risk to get caught and shot at by the enemy team.
